Optimizabilities

[partly auto-generated file]

Every risk factor is assigned an optimizability that describes how easy the factor is to optimize. For example, it is impossible to change your biological sex, but it is possible to change your alcohol intake. Therefore we have given drinking habits a higher optmizability than the biological sex.

The optimizabilities are used to decompose the risk of dying from a disease into smaller parts representing the responsibility of each risk factor. If some factors have the same optimizability, we use another method.

The different optimizabilities are

  1. It is impossible to change and it has never been in your control
  2. It is impossible to change, but it could have been the consequence of your actions
  3. It is impossible to change now, but it has once definitely been in your control
  4. It is possible to change, but it will take a lot of time.
  5. It is possible to change, and it can be changed now or very soon.
