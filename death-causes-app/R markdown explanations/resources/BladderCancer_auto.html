Fluids intake

Fluids intake is a risk factor for Bladder cancer.

Higher fluid intake decreases the risk of bladder cancer. We use the numbers from Michaud et al. (1999) who found a significant dose-response relationship. Interestingly, when the analysis was conducted separately for each type of fluid, only water was significant. However, we will not use this fact, because the other beverages may be equally beneficial, which would also make sense if the authors’ argumentation, “fluid intake is good because it increases urination and thereby bladder cancer risk”, is correct.

Because the variable Fluids intake is numeric, we have computed a smoothed approximation.
